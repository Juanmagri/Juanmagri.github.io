Water Lilies and Japanese Bridge

From Nymphéas series — 1899

Oscar Claude Monet was a French painter born in November 14th of 1840. He was a founder of impressionism, term which comes from the title of his painting Impression, Sunrise. He lived and died in Giverny at the age of 86 on 5 December.

He developed paintings in which he had the same scene with different light as the seasons evolved, something that is known as series. The most known of the named before was the Water Lilies series. Some people believed that the way he painted was because of his cataracts.
